**Feature: Buyinz Pro (Unlimited Listings & Pro Badge)**
--------------------------------------------------------

### **User Story**

As a power seller, I want to upgrade to **Buyinz Pro** so that I can bypass the six-item listing cap, manage a larger inventory, and signal my reliability to buyers via a Pro badge.

### **Prerequisites**

*   **Account Setup:** Sign in with a seller account that has exactly **6 active listings**.
    
*   **Inventory Ready:** Have photos and a title/price ready for a 7th "test" item.
    
*   **Navigation:** Ensure you are on the **Profile** tab where the "Account Card" (showing slot usage) is visible.
    

### **Instructions for the tester**

1.  **Test the Limit:** Attempt to create a new listing (7th item). Verify that the app prevents the post and directs you to an upgrade prompt or shows that your 6/6 slots are full.
    
2.  **Upgrade Process:** Navigate to the **Buyinz Pro** upgrade screen. Follow the prompts to activate the Pro subscription.
    
3.  **Verify Unlock:** Return to the listing flow and successfully post the 7th item.
    
4.  **Confirm Badge:** Go to your own **Profile** or find your listing in the **Home** feed. Verify that a **"Pro" badge** now appears next to your username.
    
5.  **Check Status:** Return to the **Profile** tab and observe the "Account Card." Confirm it no longer shows a "6/6" limit but reflects an unlimited or Pro status.
    

### **Three Salient Metrics**

1.  **Conversion Friction (The Lean Startup):** Does the transition from "Blocked" to "Pro" feel like a natural expansion of the user's business, or a technical hurdle? If the upgrade takes more than three taps, we risk losing the user's "impulse to post."
    
2.  **Perceived Professionalism (The Mom Test):** We are measuring if the Pro badge acts as a "trust signal." If a user feels "more committed" because of a badge, they are more likely to justify the subscription cost as a marketing expense.
    
3.  **Value-to-Cost Ratio:** Does the user feel that unlocking the 7th listing specifically provided enough relief to justify a monthly fee? This measures if our "Listing Cap" is set at the correct "pain point" for a budding power seller.
    

### **Three-Question Survey**

1.  **The "Pain Point" Check:** When you were blocked from posting your 7th item, did the upgrade prompt feel like a helpful next step for your business, or did it make you want to delete an old item instead?
    
2.  **Trust Perception:** If you were a buyer looking for an expensive electronic item, how much would seeing that "Pro" badge influence your choice to message that seller over someone without one?
    
3.  **Monetization Honesty:** Thinking about your own selling habits, how many items would you need to have for sale at once before you felt that paying for "Unlimited" listings was "paying for itself"?
