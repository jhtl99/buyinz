### User Story
As a buyer and seller, I want to chat within the app regarding a specific listing so that I can coordinate pickup details safely without exchanging phone numbers.

### Instructions

- As User A (Buyer): Navigate to User B's listing and tap the "Message" or "Contact Seller" button.
- Send a message proposing a meetup time and location (e.g., "Can we meet at the library at 4 PM today?").
- As User B (Seller): Open the notification or inbox, locate the new message from User A, and reply to confirm or adjust the details.
- Together: Complete the coordination entirely within the chat window, explicitly avoiding the exchange of actual phone numbers or social media handles.

### Metrics

- **Context Preservation**: Buyers and sellers shouldn't have to waste time explaining which item they are discussing. If the chat automatically links the item context, friction drops significantly.
- **Platform Retention**: The core goal is privacy. If the user feels the in-app chat is clunky or unreliable, they will immediately try to move the conversation to iMessage or WhatsApp. Satisfaction is measured by their willingness to stay in your app.
- **Logistical Confidence**: The chat isn't for socializing; it's a utility for a physical transaction. We must measure if the interface provides enough clarity (timestamps, read receipts, item details) for users to confidently execute a real-world meetup.

### Questions

- **Question 1 (Gauging Context Preservation):** "When you initiated the conversation, what extra steps did you have to take to ensure the seller knew exactly which item you wanted to buy?"
- **Question 2 (Gauging Platform Retention & Privacy):** "If you were buying this item from a complete stranger, would you feel the need to move this conversation to standard text messaging before meeting up? Why or why not?"
- **Question 3 (Gauging Logistical Confidence):** "Looking at the conversation you just had, how confident are you that both of you would actually end up at the exact same location and time without any miscommunication?"
