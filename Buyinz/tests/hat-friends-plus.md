### User Story
As a buyer, I want to scroll through a dynamic feed of items listed by my accepted network so that I can quickly and safely discover second-hand items.

### Prerequisites

1. Install and launch the Buyinz app (Expo/dev build as provided by the team).
2. Sign in with a **buyer** test account that already **follows** at least one other user who has **active sale listings**, or complete onboarding/sign-in and follow accounts until the Home feed is non-empty. If the feed shows “No listings yet,” follow more people or ask the team for a seeded account.
3. Stay on the **Home** tab (house icon). Ensure you are not relying on **Explore** for this test—Explore is location/discovery-oriented; Clarence’s story is the **network feed**.

### Instructions for the tester

1. Open **Home**. Wait for the feed to finish loading (spinner disappears).
2. Note the header control **“Include friends of friends.”** Turn it **off**, scroll through at least **five** listings (swipe vertically; each post is one full “page”). Observe image, title, price, seller name/handle, and any timestamp or social cues on each card.
3. Turn **“Include friends of friends”** **on**. Wait for the feed to refresh. Scroll again and notice whether the set of listings or ordering changes.
4. Without using a “refresh” button, continue scrolling to the end of the loaded list and observe whether **more posts load** as you go (infinite scroll) or whether you reach a clear end.
5. Tap into **one** listing to open its detail screen, then go back to the feed. Confirm you can return to browsing smoothly.
6. Optionally open **Explore** once, then return to **Home**, to compare how “anonymous / area-based” browsing feels versus the **Home** feed (you are not required to complete a purchase).

### Three salient metrics (and why they matter)

These are **actionable** signals (Lean Startup: measure behavior and learning, not vanity counts) and they are phrased around **the tester’s life and choices** (The Mom Test: avoid fishing for compliments about “our feed”).

1. **Task success — network-sourced discovery**  
   *Does the tester report that listings clearly come from people in their network (or extended network with the toggle), matching the story?*  
   This maps to **conversion funnel** thinking: if users cannot tell the feed is “theirs,” the core value proposition failed before any sale.

2. **Trust and safety in context**  
   *Does knowing who posted affect comfort, hesitation, or interest compared to anonymous marketplaces?*  
   This is a **qualitative leading indicator** for retention and transactions—closer to “risk removed” than a raw star rating.

3. **Engagement / ease of browsing**  
   *Smooth scrolling, no confusion about refresh, willingness to keep browsing* (and whether the layout feels familiar, like a social feed).  
   This aligns with **activation and engagement metrics**: friction here predicts whether people will **return** (a proxy for whether they’d pay for a product that depends on habitual browsing).

Answer in a few sentences each. Avoid “rate our app 1–5” style scores.

3. **Behavioral commitment (actionable)** — If you had ten minutes to kill tomorrow and wanted to browse second-hand stuff for fun, would you open **this app’s Home feed** first, something else first, or both? What would drive that choice?
