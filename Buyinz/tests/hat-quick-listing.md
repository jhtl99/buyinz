### User Story
As a seller, I want to quickly snap photos and list an item with minimal text so that I can post my second-hand goods for sale without it feeling like a chore.

### Prerequisites:

- The application must be installed on a mobile device (or a simulator configured with camera/photo library access).
- The user must have an active, logged-in account.
- The user needs a physical item nearby to use as a test prop (e.g., a textbook, a coffee mug, or a piece of clothing).

Instructions:

- Open the application and locate the primary action button(the '+') to create a new listing.
- Use the in-app camera feature to take a single, clear photo of the physical item you have nearby.
- In the text fields provided, enter only a 1-to-3 word title (e.g., "Blue Coffee Mug") and a price. Intentionally provide less than 5 words for each field.
- Tap the "Post Listing" button.
- Navigate to your public profile or the main marketplace feed to verify the item is now live and visible to buyers.

### Key Metrics
**Perceived Friction** The user story explicitly states the goal is to list an item "without it feeling like a chore." Measuring the user's perceived effort is critical. As highlighted in The Lean Startup, if the value hypothesis is about ease of use, we must validate that the mental hurdle to posting is virtually zero.

**Time-to-List Comparison**: Rather than measuring raw seconds, we need to measure how this experience compares to their baseline. The Mom Test warns against asking people if they "like" a feature; instead, we anchor the metric to their actual past experiences with competitors (like eBay or Facebook Marketplace) to see if we are delivering superior speed.

**Visual Communication Efficacy**: The user story explicitly pairs "snap photos" with "minimal text." For the user to be satisfied, the app must make them feel like their photos are doing the heavy lifting. If the user still feels the urge to explain the item in text, the photo-first hypothesis has failed. We need to measure if the visual output makes text feel genuinely unnecessary.

### Questions
**Question 1 (Gauging Visual Efficacy)**: "Looking at your live listing right now, if a potential buyer were to message you, what questions do you think they would ask that aren't already answered by the photo you took?"|

**Question 2 (Gauging Perceived Friction):** "Imagine you just found a box of 15 old items in your closet that you want to get rid of today. Based on the process you just went through, how would you feel about sitting down and listing all 15 items right now?" (This gets directly at the "chore" aspect without asking "was it easy?")

**Question 3 (Gauging Time/Speed relative to baseline)**: "Think about the last time you sold an item online using another platform. What was the most noticeable difference between that experience and the steps you just took here?" (This prompts them to naturally bring up speed or simplicity if it actually worked, validating the Lean Startup value hypothesis).
