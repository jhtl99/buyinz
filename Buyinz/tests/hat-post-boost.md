### User Story
As a seller, I want to pay a small fee to boost my listing so that it appears at the top of my network's feed and sells faster.

### Prerequisites

1. Install and launch Buyinz as provided by the team.
2. Sign in with a **seller** test account that **owns** at least one **active** (not sold) **sale** listing. If you only have a buyer account, ask the team to transfer a listing or use a seeded seller account.
3. Know the **listing id** or navigate to **your** listing from the feed/profile flow the team uses in their demo (you must open the **listing detail** screen for that item).

### Instructions for the tester

1. Open your listing’s **detail** screen (`/listing/[id]` in the app).
2. Confirm you see the primary call-to-action **“Boost for $1.99 · 24 hours”** (only when you are the owner and the listing is not already boosted or sold).
3. Tap it and walk through the **Listing Boost** modal: read the price and duration, complete the **mock** checkout (the team’s flow may ask for card fields or a placeholder card photo—follow on-screen instructions until you see success).
4. After success, confirm on the **same** listing page that a **“Boosted”** badge (or equivalent row) appears and that any **countdown** or time-left copy matches a **24-hour** boost window.
5. Switch to a **buyer** account (or ask a teammate) on **Home**, scroll the feed, and check whether **this** listing appears **higher** or **more prominently** than comparable non-boosted listings near the same time (per product rules: boosted items prioritized). Note what you observe.
6. **Optional (if time permits):** After the boost window ends (or using a test harness the team provides), confirm the listing **no longer** shows as boosted and ranks like a normal listing.

### Three salient metrics (and why they matter)

1. **Discoverability and comprehension of the offer**  
   *Can the seller find Boost, and do they paraphrase what they get (price, duration, visibility) without prompting?*  
   Lean Startup: this is the **activation** step for monetization—if the value isn’t understood, payment is noise.

2. **Confidence that the product “did something”**  
   *Badge on listing + observable position in the network feed after boost.*  
   This is an **outcome metric** (not vanity revenue): it validates the **mechanism** sellers pay for.

3. **Willingness to pay again (retention of monetization)**  
   *Would they boost another listing in a similar situation?*  
   Maps to **repeat purchase / LTV** thinking for a small SKU ($1.99 boost)—the closest honest proxy to “they’d spend money on this service.”

### Three-question survey (User Story 2)

Short paragraph answers preferred.

1. **Clarity (not “was it easy?”)** — Before you tapped pay, how would you explain **in your own words** what the boost was supposed to do for your listing, and what—if anything—was unclear until you tried it?

2. **Evidence (outcome)** — After you finished, what **in the app** convinced you the boost was active? If you checked the feed as a buyer, what did you notice about where your listing showed up?

3. **Future money (Mom Test–style)** — Imagine you have another item to sell next month, similar to this one. Would you pay for a boost again, skip it, or only use it in some cases? Describe the **situation** where you’d pay vs skip—avoid saying what you think the team wants to hear.
